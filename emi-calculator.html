What is EMI?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Installment. It is the fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs are used to pay off both interest and principal each month, so that over a specified number of years, the loan is fully paid off along with interest.

How is EMI Calculated?

The EMI calculation formula is based on three main factors:

EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N]/[(1+R)^N-1]

  • P = Principal loan amount
  • R = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • N = Loan tenure in months

This formula ensures that your monthly payment remains the same throughout the loan tenure, with the proportion of interest and principal varying over time.

EMI Calculator FAQs

What factors affect my EMI amount?

The three main factors that determine your EMI are: the principal loan amount, the interest rate, and the loan tenure. Higher loan amounts or interest rates will increase your EMI, while longer tenures will reduce your EMI (but increase total interest paid).

Is a higher EMI or longer tenure better?

This depends on your financial situation. A higher EMI for a shorter tenure means you pay less total interest but need higher monthly cash flow. A lower EMI for longer tenure reduces monthly burden but increases total interest paid. Choose based on what you can comfortably afford.

Can I change my EMI after taking a loan?

Some lenders offer EMI restructuring options where you can request to increase or decrease your EMI. However, this usually involves fees and may extend your loan tenure. Some loans also allow prepayment which can reduce your EMI or tenure.

Does EMI include all charges?

EMI typically includes principal and interest components. Other charges like insurance, processing fees, or taxes may be separate. Always check the loan agreement for complete details of what's included in your EMI.
