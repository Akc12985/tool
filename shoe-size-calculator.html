How Our Shoe Size Calculator Works

Our advanced shoe size calculator uses anthropometric data and statistical correlations between height, age, and gender to estimate your most likely shoe size. While foot length is the primary determinant of shoe size, research shows that height can be a good predictor when actual foot measurements aren't available.

The algorithm adjusts for gender differences (men typically have larger feet at the same height) and age-related changes (foot size tends to stabilize in adulthood but may change later in life). The results are then converted to your preferred sizing system (US, UK, EU, or centimeters).

Shoe Fit Tips

  • Measure both feet - Most people have one foot slightly larger than the other. Always fit to your larger foot.
  • Shop late in the day - Feet swell throughout the day, so shop when your feet are at their largest.
  • Wear appropriate socks - Bring the type of socks you'll wear with the shoes when trying them on.
  • Check toe room - There should be about a thumb's width (1/2 inch) between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.
  • Walk around - Don't just stand in new shoes. Walk to check for proper fit and comfort.
  • Consider width - If shoes feel tight, you might need a wider size rather than a longer one.
